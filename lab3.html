Section 1

A "sundae ice cream" is a dessert made with one or more scoops of ice cream topped with various sweet toppings like syrup, whipped cream, nuts, fruit, or sprinkles, essentially elevating a simple scoop of ice cream into a more elaborate treat; the name "sundae" likely originated from the idea of serving this special dessert on Sundays, with the spelling altered to avoid direct religious connotations.

Section 2

"Cone ice cream" simply refers to ice cream served inside a cone-shaped, edible wafer container, essentially meaning the ice cream is held within a crispy, cone-like pastry that you can eat alongside the ice cream itself; it's the classic way to enjoy ice cream without a bowl or spoon.

Section 3

Ice cream cups are containers that hold ice cream and other frozen treats. They come in various sizes and can be used for single servings, snacks, or group gatherings.
